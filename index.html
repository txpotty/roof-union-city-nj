Recognizing Roofing Problems

Identifying roofing issues early can save you significant time and money. Here are some common signs that indicate you might need our roofing services.

Leaking Roof

A leaking roof is one of the most obvious signs of a problem. Water stains on your ceiling or walls, damp spots, or the sound of dripping water all indicate a breach in your roof’s protective barrier.

Why it Matters: Ignoring a leak can lead to significant water damage, including mold growth, rot, and structural damage. Our team will quickly locate and repair the source of the leak, preventing further harm to your property.

Missing or Damaged Shingles

Missing, cracked, curled, or granular shingles are signs of deterioration and compromise your roof’s ability to protect your home.

Why it Matters: Damaged shingles expose the underlayment to the elements, which can lead to leaks and further damage. Replacing damaged shingles promptly is essential for maintaining the integrity of your roof.

Sagging Roof

A sagging roof indicates structural problems that require immediate attention. Sagging can be caused by water damage, rot, or inadequate support.

Why it Matters: A sagging roof is a serious issue that can lead to collapse. Ignoring it can put your property and your family at risk. Our team can assess the structural integrity of your roof and provide the necessary repairs to prevent further damage.

Granules in Gutters

The presence of asphalt granules in your gutters is a sign that your shingles are deteriorating. Over time, shingles lose granules, which accumulate in the gutters.

Why it Matters: Excessive granule loss reduces the effectiveness of your shingles and shortens their lifespan. If you notice a significant amount of granules in your gutters, it may be time to consider a roof replacement.

Damaged Flashing

Flashing is the metal material used to seal joints and seams in your roof, such as around chimneys, vents, and skylights. Damaged or corroded flashing can allow water to penetrate your roof.

Why it Matters: Damaged flashing is a common cause of leaks. Our team can repair or replace damaged flashing, ensuring a weathertight seal around these vulnerable areas.

Call Now: (201) 534-3840

Mold or Moss Growth

The presence of mold or moss on your roof can indicate trapped moisture and inadequate ventilation. These growths can damage your shingles and underlying structure.

Why it Matters: Mold and moss can retain moisture, which can lead to rot and deterioration of your roofing materials. Removing these growths and addressing the underlying moisture issue is essential for maintaining a healthy roof.

Increased Energy Bills

If you notice an increase in your energy bills, it could be due to poor insulation or ventilation in your attic. A damaged roof can allow heat to escape in the winter and enter in the summer, increasing your energy consumption.

Why it Matters: An energy-efficient roof can save you money on your utility bills and improve the comfort of your home. Our team can assess your insulation and ventilation and recommend solutions to improve your home’s energy efficiency.

Attic Issues

Problems within your attic space are often indicative of roof concerns. Things like musty odors, damp spots, or visible sunlight shining through are all warning signs.

Why it matters: These factors signify that your roof is compromised, letting in moisture and potentially inviting pests. Addressing these attic-level concerns will prevent additional damages on the interior of your home and prolong the life of your roofing structure.

Age of your Roof

Even without noticeable damage, an aging roof is still a reason for concern. Depending on the materials used, most roofs decline with age, increasing the likelihood of problems.

Why it matters: As a roof ages, it becomes more prone to failures, such as cracked, missing, or severely weakened shingles. Regularly scheduled inspections can help determine when it ‘s more economical to replace an aging roof than to continue repeated repairs.

What is the average lifespan of an asphalt shingle roof?

The lifespan of an asphalt shingle roof typically ranges from 15 to 30 years, depending on the quality of the shingles, climate conditions, and proper installation. Regular maintenance and prompt repairs can extend the life of your roof.

What are the benefits of metal roofing?

Metal roofing offers several advantages, including durability, longevity, energy efficiency, and resistance to fire, wind, and pests. Metal roofs can last 50 years or more with proper maintenance.
